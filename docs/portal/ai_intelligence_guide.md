# AI Intelligence Guide

## Purpose

This guide summarizes the deterministic AI intelligence layers used by PortMap-AI.

## What Operators Should Know

PortMap-AI uses deterministic metadata-derived intelligence, not hidden autonomous action. The AI workspace combines probabilistic application attribution, learning profiles, behavior graph models, graph insights, historical risk evolution, explanation summaries, threat prediction, federation metadata, and investigation chains.

## Key Concepts

- Attribution outputs include candidates, confidence, evidence, missing evidence, and explanation metadata.
- Learning profiles summarize repeated observations, stability, drift, recommendations, and confidence evolution.
- Behavior graph layers model relationships, clusters, temporal evolution, insights, review queues, and investigation guidance.
- Threat prediction remains advisory and deterministic.
- Federated intelligence shares metadata summaries, not payloads, credentials, secrets, or policy files.

## Safety Notes

AI intelligence is advisory. It does not enforce policy, block traffic, terminate processes, execute remediation, mutate learning models online, contact external AI services, or inspect payloads.

## Current Limitations

The system does not perform machine-learning training or LLM inference for decisions. Current intelligence is deterministic and metadata-first.
